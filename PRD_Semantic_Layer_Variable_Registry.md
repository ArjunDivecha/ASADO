## 0. One-paragraph summary

ASADO holds ~2,000 variables across DuckDB + Neo4j. The metadata that exists today describes a variable's *shape and freshness* (counts, dates, sparse/stale flags) but not its *meaning* ÔÇö definition, units, sign, point-in-time availability, cross-country comparability, economic mechanism, or known data-quality issues. This PRD builds a **structured, machine-readable variable registry** that is the single source of truth for that missing semantic layer, lives inside the database, is validated by CI so it cannot drift, and renders a human-readable dictionary as a downstream view. It is the prerequisite for two later efforts (extending the cleaning pipeline to the econ block, and PIT-correcting the macro data), and the foundation for handing the database to a frontier model that must reason over the inputs without a human in the loop.

---

## 1. Problem statement

A model (or a new senior researcher) handed this database cannot currently answer, for an arbitrary variable: *what does this number mean, in what units, when was it knowable in real time, which direction is "good," why might it predict returns, and can I trust it?*

Today's metadata:
- `variable_meta` (DuckDB table, ~654 rows) ÔÇö covers only the daily + commodity + predmkt surfaces; columns are structural (source_table, frequency, category, optimizer flag, freshness). The 7 monthly macro tables and `gdelt_panel`'s 1,323 vars have no row.
- `variable_catalog.json` (auto-generated by `build_schema_registry.py`) ÔÇö per variable: source, row/country counts, dates, freshness, sparse/stale/forecast flags, a normalization tag. Good coverage of *facts*, no *semantics*.
- `DATA_DICTIONARY.md` ÔÇö human prose, and **partly fictional**: it names `feature_panel` and `normalized_panel` as the primary surfaces, but the live DB has neither (per `ASADO_DATABASE_MAP.md`). A stale dictionary the model believes is worse than none.

The gap splits cleanly:
- **Sanity axis** (definition, units, sign, quality, comparability) ÔÇö missing, fixable by documentation.
- **Temporal / PIT axis** (publication lag, revision-prone, vintage availability) ÔÇö missing, and the most dangerous because cleaning and z-scoring make data *look* trustworthy without making it *knowable-at-the-time*.

---

## 2. Objective

Build a **variable registry**: one canonical, machine-readable metadata record per variable, composed of two layers that are never mixed:

1. **Auto-generated facts** ÔÇö regenerated by scanning the live DB. Never hand-edited.
2. **Curated semantics** ÔÇö authored in a version-controlled seed file, loaded into the DB. The missing half.

Joined in a view; rendered to a human dictionary; guarded by CI.

### Success criteria (definition of done)
- Every live *input* variable has exactly one registry row (CI-enforced).
- Auto-facts and curated semantics join cleanly on the variable key.
- Tier-A variables (signal-driving + econ block) have human-verified semantics including units, comparability, sign, mechanism, publication lag, and revision flag.
- A CI guardrail fails the build if (a) a live variable lacks a registry row, or (b) the registry/dictionary references a DB object that does not exist.
- The human-readable dictionary is generated *from* the registry, not maintained by hand.
- Two reports are produced: a doc-vs-live mismatch report, and a suspect-value report (out-of-range observations).

---

## 3. Non-goals (explicitly out of scope for this PRD)

- **No change to the data warehouse schema or contents.** This is metadata about existing variables, not a re-architecture. (One exception: writing the new registry table and its view.)
- **No PIT *backfill*.** We *record* each series' publication lag and revision behavior; we do not reconstruct historical vintages here. (Prospective vintage capture and ALFRED/archived-WEO pulls are a separate downstream effort, scoped by this registry.)
- **No extension of the cleaning pipeline.** That is downstream and depends on this registry's `cross_country_comparable` and `frequency` fields.
- **No new alpha model.** This is infrastructure.
- **No hand-authoring of the 1,323 GDELT theme/gcam definitions.** Those are handled by a generated crosswalk (┬º6, Tier C).

---

## 4. Guardrails for the executing agent

1. **Trust the live DB, not the docs.** `DATA_DICTIONARY.md`, `README.md`, `CLAUDE.md`, and `AGENTS.md` are partly stale. Derive the variable universe and all facts by querying `Data/asado.duckdb` directly. Where a doc disagrees with the live DB, the live DB wins and the disagreement goes in the mismatch report.
2. **Read-only by default; back up before any write.** Open the DB read-only for inspection. The only writes are the new `variable_registry` table, its view, and (Phase 4) Neo4j edges. Back up `asado.duckdb` before the first write.
3. **Respect the returns-first guardrail.** The registry documents input/explanatory variables. Optimizer outputs (`factor_returns*`, `factor_top20_membership`, attribution) are returns surfaces, tagged as such, never treated as inputs.
4. **Separate generated from authored.** Never hand-edit the auto-facts layer. Never let a build step overwrite curated semantics. The two meet only in a join view.
5. **Flag confidence; do not bluff.** For every curated field you draft (especially mechanism, publication lag, revision-prone, units, comparability), record a `review_status` of `model_drafted` and never `verified`. Only a human sets `verified`. Where you don't know, write `unknown`, not a guess.
6. **Use the project venv** (`venv/bin/python`, duckdb 1.5.x) from the repo root.

## 5. Registry schema

**Grain:** one row per concrete variable string (what actually gets queried), but author semantics **once per base concept** and propagate to its `_CS`/`_TS` variants ÔÇö do not re-write the mechanism three times.

### 5a. Curated semantics (version-controlled seed ÔåÆ loaded into DB)
Source of truth: a git-tracked `config/variable_registry_seed.yaml` (mirrors the existing `config/event_log_seed.yaml` pattern). Loaded into table `variable_registry`.

| Field | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `variable` | str (PK) | exact string as stored |
| `base_variable` | str | un-suffixed concept; links `_CS`/`_TS` variants |
| `normalization` | enum | `raw` / `_CS` / `_TS` / `other` |
| `definition` | str | one plain sentence |
| `source_provider` | str | IMF / BIS / World Bank / Bloomberg / GDELT / WB Pink Sheet / ÔÇª |
| `source_series_id` | str | the specific dataset/series identifier at the provider |
| `units` | str | %, bps, USD, ratio, `2010=100`, z-score, ÔÇª |
| `cross_country_comparable` | bool | ratio/comparable vs scale/level ÔÇö **drives the cleaner's cross-sectional test** |
| `native_frequency` | enum | `D` / `M` / `Q` / `A` |
| `publication_lag` | str | period_end ÔåÆ availability (e.g. "+45d", "+1 release") or an `available_at` rule |
| `revision_prone` | bool | does the provider revise history? |
| `vintage_available` | enum | `none` / `ALFRED` / `archived_release` / `other` |
| `sign` | enum | `higher_is_better` / `lower_is_better` (the ~19 flips) |
| `economic_mechanism` | str | one line: hypothesized why-it-predicts |
| `mechanism_reference` | str | paper / source, where applicable |
| `concept` | enum | valuation / momentum / growth / monetary / external / sentiment / structural / commodity / network / technical |
| `is_broadcast` | bool | global print (same across all countries) vs country-specific |
| `quality_flag` | enum | `clean` / `suspect` / `known_error` |
| `valid_range` | str | expected min/max (catches the 110,000 M2 type) |
| `review_status` | enum | `verified` / `model_drafted` / `needs_review` / `unknown` |
| `notes` | str | free text |

### 5b. Auto-generated facts (scanned from live DB; not in the seed)
Extend / reuse `build_schema_registry.py`: `source_table`, `row_count`, `country_count`, `first_date`, `last_date`, `freshness_days`, `is_stale`, `is_sparse`, `is_forecast`, `dtype`, `observed_min`, `observed_max`, `null_pct`.

### 5c. Join + render
- View `variable_registry_full` = curated Ô¿Ø auto-facts on `variable`.
- `DATA_DICTIONARY.md` (and any agent-facing JSON) is **regenerated from** `variable_registry_full`. The markdown is a view; the table is the source of truth.

## 6. Roadmap

### Phase 0 ÔÇö Inventory & reconciliation (no semantics yet)
- Enumerate the true variable universe from the live DB (distinct variables across `unified_panel` + the daily tables + predmkt; tag returns surfaces separately).
- Decide and document the grain (per concrete variable, with `base_variable` linkage; confirm the `_CS`/`_TS` propagation rule).
- Regenerate the auto-facts layer for the full universe.
- Produce **`reports/doc_vs_live_mismatch.md`**: every place the docs reference a non-existent object (`feature_panel`, `normalized_panel`), wrong counts, or stale ranges. Recommend retire/fix actions.
- **Acceptance:** universe enumerated and counted; grain decided; auto-facts cover 100% of the universe; mismatch report delivered.

### Phase 1 ÔÇö Schema + scaffolding (registry exists, semantics empty)
- Create `config/variable_registry_seed.yaml` (empty rows for every variable) and the loader ÔåÆ `variable_registry` table.
- Mechanically populate the deterministic curated fields: `base_variable`, `normalization`, `is_broadcast`, `sign` (extract the actual flip list from the code/data ÔÇö do not guess), `native_frequency` (from existing meta where present).
- Build the `variable_registry_full` join view.
- Build the **CI guardrail** (model on `validate_returns_first.py`): fail if a live variable has no registry row; fail if the registry or rendered dictionary references a non-existent DB object.
- Build the markdown renderer (table ÔåÆ dictionary).
- **Acceptance:** registry covers 100% of the universe (rows may be semantically empty); deterministic fields populated; CI green on coverage and object-existence; renderer produces a dictionary that matches the live DB.

### Phase 2 ÔÇö Curated semantics, tiered drafting
Draft `definition`, `units`, `cross_country_comparable`, `sign`, `economic_mechanism`, `concept`; mark every drafted field `model_drafted`.
- **Tier A (do first):** variables that feed or could feed signals ÔÇö the 8 live optimizer factors' base concepts, the T2 ~53, and the **econ/macro block** (`bloomberg_factors`, `imf_factors`, `external_factors`, `extended_factors`, `macrostructure_factors`, `demographics_dip`, WB commodities).
- **Tier B:** the remaining dense panels.
- **Tier C:** the 1,323 GDELT `theme_*`/`gcam_*` vars ÔÇö **generate a crosswalk** from the clipped names back to the GKG/GCAM codebook; author one mechanism per theme *family*, not per variant. No hand-authoring.
- **Acceptance:** Tier A fully drafted and flagged `needs_review`; B and C delivered per scope; coverage report by tier and by `review_status`.

### Phase 3 ÔÇö PIT & quality enrichment
- Populate `publication_lag`, `revision_prone`, `vintage_available` per source. Research each provider's release cadence and revision behavior; explicitly flag series stored as latest-revised with no vintage (IMF IFS/BOP, World Bank WDI/WGI, BIS credit gap, OECD CLI), and the clean cases (market series, GDELT, Pink Sheet prices, EPU/GPR, archived WEO).
- Populate `quality_flag` / `valid_range`; cross-check against live data and auto-detect out-of-range observations ÔåÆ **`reports/suspect_values.md`** (e.g., Vietnam `BBG_M2_YoY` Ôëê 110,000; `UNDP_HDI` 93ÔÇô130; `MS_CentralBank_*` in the hundreds of thousands; `iNATGAS` mislabeled; the `yoy_pct` / `ret_12m_pct` alias collision).
- **Acceptance:** PIT fields populated for all Tier-A sources with confidence flags; suspect-value report delivered.

### Phase 4 ÔÇö Graph lineage (optional / stretch)
- Push `Variable ÔåÆ Concept ÔåÆ DataSource` nodes and `derived_from`, `normalized_variant_of`, `alternative_measure_of` edges into Neo4j, so correlated measures of the same underlying are discoverable and the model doesn't double-count them.
- **Acceptance:** lineage and equivalence queryable in Neo4j; round-trips to the registry.

## 7. Key risk: convincing-but-wrong metadata

The agent will be **confidently wrong** on exactly the highest-value fields ÔÇö `economic_mechanism`, `publication_lag`, `revision_prone`, `units`, and `cross_country_comparable` ÔÇö because they require domain and provider knowledge it will happily fabricate. Mitigations, mandatory:
- Every drafted curated field carries `review_status = model_drafted` until a human sets `verified`.
- Human checkpoint after Phase 2 Tier A and after Phase 3: Arjun reviews mechanisms, comparability, and PIT fields before anything downstream consumes them.
- The registry must never silently upgrade its own confidence; `verified` is set only by a human edit to the seed.
