# Outside View: What the Literature & Practice Know About Country Selection and Country-ETF Trading

**Evidence miner 5 — literature/practitioner survey, 2026-07-02.**
Purpose: cross-check ASADO's internal validated-signal digest (harness v2.1, 2026-06-12) against published academic and practitioner evidence, and identify importable ideas for a long-only + cash, $100k IBKR book trading 34 US-listed country ETFs.

Evidence labels used throughout:
- **STRONG** — peer-reviewed, replicated across samples/authors, or large out-of-sample record.
- **MODERATE** — one good paper or consistent practitioner evidence; some out-of-sample support.
- **WEAK/FOLKLORE** — widely believed, thin or conflicting evidence, or contradicted live.

## 1. Country equity rotation factors — what survives costs (2015–2026 view)

### 1.1 Cross-country momentum — STRONG gross, MODERATE net, decaying

- The canonical result: 12-month (and 6-month) trailing-return sorts across country indices earn a significant spread; Quantpedia's replication of the classic country-momentum strategy reports roughly **~5%/yr excess gross**, and notes ETF bid-ask spreads sit "markedly below the implied break-even transaction cost levels," so the effect plausibly survives realistic ETF costs at monthly rebalance ([Quantpedia — Momentum Factor Effect in Country Equity Indexes](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/momentum-factor-effect-in-country-equity-indexes)).
- Comprehensive review: Zaremba's survey of the cross-section of country equity returns (78+ country markets, 100+ studies) confirms momentum, value and size at the country level, with effects **stronger in small/medium markets than in large liquid ones** ([MDPI review, JRFM 2019](https://www.mdpi.com/1911-8074/12/4/165)).
- **The single most important costs paper for this project**: Zaremba & Andreu, *"Paper profits or real money? Trading costs and stock market anomalies in country ETFs"* (International Review of Financial Analysis, 2018). They replicate **120 country-level anomalies** across 42 country indices 1996–2017 using actual ETF price and spread data. Result: 46% of long-only monthly-rebalanced anomaly portfolios show significant gross alpha (concentrated in **value, momentum, liquidity**), but **"the effect of transaction costs proves largely lethal,"** leaving only a handful profitable. Rescue levers that work: **less frequent rebalancing (annual restores ~0.44%/mo average alpha on long-only portfolios), cap-weighting, and low-cost securities** ([SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3038514), [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1057521918300395)).
  - Note the structure of this finding: it condemns the *middle frequency* (monthly rebalance of modest-IC signals at full turnover), not fast signals with tiny per-trade costs or slow signals with tiny turnover. That is exactly the barbell ASADO's own cost grid found (1d holds win net for strong fast signals; 21d holds rescue slow signals; nothing survives 25bps).
- **Factor momentum across countries** is a newer, credible wrinkle: Fieberg, Metko & Zaremba, *"Cross-Country Factor Momentum"* (Economics Letters, 2024) — factors that performed well in one country/recently continue to; related work finds short-term factor momentum survives costs at the factor-portfolio level ([Alpha Architect summary](https://alphaarchitect.com/cross-country-factor-momentum/)).
- Caveat: country momentum is one of the older published anomalies (Asness–Liew–Stevens 1997; Bhojraj–Swaminathan 2006), and the general post-publication decay result applies (Section 3). Post-2010 country momentum spreads are visibly thinner than the 1980–2005 sample that made the anomaly famous. Verdict: use it, but haircut historical Sharpe by ~half.

### 1.2 Value / CAPE spreads — STRONG at 10-year horizons, WEAK as a 1-year timing/rotation signal, and famously poor live

- CAPE robustly predicts **long-horizon (10y+)** country returns in-sample and cross-country ([Barclays–Shiller research program](https://indices.cib.barclays/IM/21/en/indices/static/shiller.app); [Quantpedia — CAPE effect within countries](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/value-factor-effect-within-countries)).
- Research Affiliates ("CAPE Fatigue", Arnott et al.) show rolling out-of-sample CAPE-based predictions are far weaker than the in-sample fit suggests, and warn against using CAPE for market timing ([Research Affiliates](https://www.researchaffiliates.com/publications/articles/618-cape-fatigue)).
- **The decisive live datapoint: Cambria GVAL** (Meb Faber's Global Value ETF, launched March 2014, buys the cheapest-CAPE quartile of countries). Backtests of the strategy showed **15.9–17.6%/yr compounded 1980–2013**; the live fund has delivered roughly **2.4%/yr since inception (to 1/2025)** and underperformed plain IXUS ([Cambria fund page](https://www.cambriafunds.com/gval), [stockanalysis.com/etf/gval](https://stockanalysis.com/etf/gval/), [Faber's own profile](https://mebfaber.com/2020/09/28/cambria-fund-profile-series-cambria-global-value-etf-gval/)). Twelve years is long enough to be informative: cheap-country CAPE rotation as a *standalone* strategy has been a live failure for a full decade+, largely because it is structurally short the US/short quality and the valuation gap kept widening.
- AQR's view (Asness–Ilmanen–Villalon, *"Exceptional Expectations: US vs non-US equities"*, 2023): most US outperformance since 1990 is **relative CAPE re-rating**, not fundamentals — i.e., value spreads are informative about *expected* returns but the convergence timing is unknowable ([AQR whitepaper](https://www.aqr.com/-/media/AQR/Documents/Whitepapers/Exceptional-Expectations-US-vs-Non-US-Equities.pdf?sc_lang=en)).
- **Corroborates ASADO's internal DEAD verdict on valuation percentiles (CAPE/PB/DY 10y percentile) as cross-sectional return signals at daily–monthly horizons.** The literature only supports value as (a) a very-slow-turnover tilt blended with momentum, or (b) a strategic (multi-year) allocation anchor. Asness et al., *"Market Timing: Sin a Little"* (2017, JPM) is the standard reference for "value alone times badly; value+momentum together is usable in small doses" (from memory of the AQR literature — MODERATE label on the exact citation, STRONG on the finding).

### 1.3 FX carry / interest-rate differentials as an equity country signal — MODERATE, mostly indirect

- Koijen–Moskowitz–Pedersen–Vrugt "Carry" (JFE 2018) established that carry (futures-implied) predicts returns **within every asset class including equity indices**; consistently positive, significant coefficients ([Macrosynergy replication of equity index future carry](https://macrosynergy.com/academy/notebooks/equity-index-future-carry/)). But note: equity "carry" there is dividend-implied, not FX carry.
- FX carry *as a cross-sectional equity-country* signal is much thinner. Zaremba, Cakici, Bianchi & Long, *"Interest Rate Changes and the Cross-Section of Global Equity Returns"* (SSRN 3756047) find interest-rate *changes* predict country equity returns; the *level* differential (classic carry) mostly loads on EM risk premia. ([SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3756047))
- The interesting recent result runs the **other way**: the *equity differential factor* — trailing relative equity performance — predicts the cross-section of **currency** returns and subsumes carry (FAJ 2020, [Tandfonline](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0015198X.2020.1712924)).
- Verdict for the book: FX carry is not a first-tier country-equity signal; ASADO's finding that FX vol / RR / carry surfaces are DEAD as equity signals is **consistent with the published record** (FX-derived signals predict FX, weakly equities).

### 1.4 Macro momentum / economic trend — STRONG (long-sample, multi-asset), the best-documented "fundamental" country signal

- Brooks, *"A Half Century of Macro Momentum"* (AQR 2017) and Brooks–Feilbogen–Ooi–Akant, *"Economic Trend"* (AQR whitepaper Apr 2023; SSRN Feb 2024): go long assets (including **country equity indices**) with improving macro fundamentals (growth, inflation, international trade, monetary policy, risk sentiment trends), short deteriorating. Consistent risk-adjusted returns over 50+ years, pervasive across markets and measures, low correlation to traditional premia, good drawdown behavior ([AQR 2017 PDF](https://www.aqr.com/-/media/AQR/Documents/Insights/White-Papers/A-Half-Century-of-Macro-Momentum.pdf), [Economic Trend PDF](https://www.aqr.com/-/media/AQR/Documents/Whitepapers/Economic-Trend_.pdf?sc_lang=en), [SSRN 4710868](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4710868)).
- Country-level corroboration: Long, Zaremba et al., *"Macroeconomics matter: Leading economic indicators and the cross-section of global stock returns"* (Journal of Financial Markets, 2022) — **changes in the OECD composite leading indicator predict the cross-section of country returns** ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386418122000295)).
- These are monthly-frequency, low-turnover signals — precisely the class that survives Zaremba–Andreu's cost test. ASADO's warehouse (OECD CLI, PMI, WEO, ECFC revisions, inflation surprises) already contains the raw ingredients. **This is the strongest literature endorsement for building a slow macro-trend sleeve alongside the fast market-derived combiner.**

### 1.5 Seasonality / turn-of-month — STRONG for its size, and unusually well-suited to long-only + cash

- Turn-of-month (ToM): McConnell & Xu (FAJ 2008) show the last trading day + first 3 days of the month account for essentially **all** equity returns, in the US **and 30+ international markets**, persisting post-1987. Kunkel–Compton–Beyer (2003): 4-day ToM window across 19 countries. Chen, Chou & Ko (2015 study of **international iShares country ETFs** 1996–2012): ToM daily returns of **15–20 bps vs ~0 on other days** — i.e., the effect exists in the exact instruments this book trades ([ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/339080461_Return-enhancing_strategies_with_international_ETFs_Exploiting_the_turn-of-the-month_effect), [Quantpedia ToM](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/turn-of-the-month-in-equity-indexes), [QuantSeeker 2024 re-test](https://www.quantseeker.com/p/turn-of-the-month-strategies-do-they)).
- Recent re-tests (2024–2026) find it persists at ~10bps/day in the window across markets, with a proposed mechanism of month-end institutional rebalancing / pension inflows and "infrequent rebalancing, risk deferral" ([JIMF 2026 ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1042443126000259)).
- Why it matters here: ToM is a **timing** (cash↔equity) effect, not a cross-sectional one — it is natively long-only + cash, ~24 round trips/year, and its per-trade edge (40–80bps per window) is an order of magnitude above ETF spreads for the liquid half of the universe. FOLKLORE risk: some recent US-only tests show attenuation; the international ETF evidence is more robust.
- Broader seasonality (same-calendar-month "seasonality effect" across world indices, Keloharju-style) is MODERATE ([Quantpedia](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/market-seasonality-effect-in-world-equity-indexes)).

### 1.6 Machine learning at the country level — MODERATE, converges to the same variables

- *"What drives stock returns across countries? Insights from machine learning models"* (International Review of Financial Analysis, 2024) — first ML treatment of the cross-section of **country** equity returns; top predictors correspond to the familiar families (momentum, reversal, valuation ratios) ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1057521924005015)).
- *"Machine learning goes global"* (JEDC 2023) — cross-sectional ML predictability replicates internationally; predictions load on value, size, momentum, short-term reversal ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0165188923001318)).
- Sobering meta-result: *"The Expected Returns on Machine-Learning Strategies"* (2024/25, AFA) — estimated premia drop ~30% out-of-sample, attributed to **overfitting more than crowding** ([AFA](https://afajof.org/management/viewp.php?n=75544)). Directly relevant discount factor for ASADO's in-sample-selected ridge combiner.

## 2. International ETF microstructure at retail scale

### 2.1 NAV premium/discount mean reversion — STRONG phenomenon, MODERATE net tradability

- Petajisto, *"Inefficiencies in the Pricing of Exchange-Traded Funds"* (FAJ 2017): ETF prices oscillate around NAV in a band averaging **~200bps for international/illiquid-underlying funds**; even after controlling for stale NAVs using a **cross-section of similar ETFs as the fair-value benchmark**, ~100bps of genuine mispricing remains; trading strategies exploiting it earn "substantial abnormal returns **before transaction costs**," proving short-term mean reversion in ETF price-vs-value ([paper PDF](http://www.petajisto.net/papers/etf28.pdf), [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2000336)).
- Key implementation insight from Petajisto: naive premium/discount (price vs stale NAV) is NOT the signal — for Asia-domiciled ETFs the published NAV is up to 15 hours stale. The robust version compares an ETF's price to a **peer-group fair value** (e.g., EWY vs a basket of correlated Asia ETFs + futures-implied moves).
- Deviations can persist for days in international funds (limits to arbitrage), so this is a 1–5 day mean-reversion trade, not an intraday one.
- Retail feasibility: the premium band is big relative to the ~2–15bps spreads on liquid country ETFs, but for thin ones (EPHE, VNM, EPOL tail) spreads/creation costs are precisely *why* the premium exists — the edge nets down fast outside the liquid half ([Vanguard on premiums/discounts](https://corporate.vanguard.com/content/corporatesite/us/en/corp/articles/etf-premiums-and-discounts-explained.html); [Natixis on spreads as cost](https://www.im.natixis.com/en-us/insights/portfolio-construction/2024/etf-cost-bid-ask-spread)). Best retail use: **as an entry/exit filter on trades you were going to do anyway** (buy at discount, delay buys at premium), which monetizes the band without standalone-arb turnover.

### 2.2 Stale-pricing / time-zone arbitrage — STRONG historically (mutual funds), now structurally different in ETFs

- The classic literature: Chalmers–Edelen–Kadlec (2001), Boudoukh–Richardson–Subrahmanyam–Whitelaw (2002), Zitzewitz (2003), Goetzmann–Ivković–Rouwenhorst — international **mutual fund** NAVs were computed off home-market closes, so late-day S&P moves predicted next-day fund NAV changes; annualized arb profits of 35–70% were documented before fair-value pricing rules (2004+) killed it ([UN/ESCAP survey chapter](https://www.unescap.org/sites/default/d8files/11-CHA~1_0_0.PDF), [Duke honors replication on Vanguard funds](https://sites.duke.edu/econhonors/files/2008/03/donnelly.original.pdf)).
- In **US-listed country ETFs**, the same information asymmetry exists but the *market price* (not a stale NAV) is the tradable object, and market makers already fair-value it continuously off S&P futures, FX, and correlated ETFs. Early evidence of exploitable next-day cross-autocorrelations in iShares country funds (Tse et al., *"Exploitable cross autocorrelations among iShares"*) dates from the 2000s and decayed as MM models improved ([ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/228723991_Exploitable_cross_autocorrelations_among_iShares)).
- **Direct evidence on what remains**: Levy & Lieberman, *"Overreaction of country ETFs to US market returns"* (Journal of Banking & Finance, 2013) — during **non-synchronized hours** (home market closed), country ETF prices are driven predominantly by the S&P 500, with a beta that **exceeds** the true long-run beta of the underlying index to the US — i.e., US-listed country ETFs **systematically over-embed the US move** while the home market is closed, and this over-reaction **reverses** subsequently. During synchronized hours prices track NAV ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0378426612000866)).
  - This is the critical outside answer to ASADO's LL_LEADER_GAP_5D tradability trap: the literature agrees the US→local-index lead-lag (Rapach–Strauss–Zhou, JF 2013, robust out-of-sample, [SSRN](https://ssrn.com/abstract=1508484)) is real but **already (over-)priced into US-listed follower ETFs by the US close**. The tradable residual in ETF space is therefore **contrarian**: fade the US-hours overshoot in Asia/Europe ETFs, not follow it. An arXiv study of asynchronous ADRs documents the same overnight-vs-intraday reversal structure ([arXiv 1611.03110](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1611.03110)).
- Related: Filippou & Gozluklu, *"ETF Arbitrage and International Diversification"* — ETF-level arbitrage activity in country funds affects comovement/diversification ([AEA](https://www.aeaweb.org/conference/2022/preliminary/paper/HBBydZy8)).

### 2.3 Overnight vs intraday splits — MODERATE and mechanism-dependent; one loud live failure

- For US equities, the "night effect" (returns accrue overnight; ~75% of SPX and >100% of Russell 2000 returns overnight over 20 years — Lou–Polk–Skouras JFE 2019 lineage) was famous enough to spawn dedicated ETFs. **NightShares (NSPY/NIWM) launched June 2022 and liquidated August 2023** after the effect inverted immediately ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-07-19/-night-effect-funds-to-shut-down-with-overnight-returns-elusive), [etf.com](https://www.etf.com/sections/news/2-nightshares-etfs-close-after-struggling-gain-traction)). Cautionary tale: an unconditional overnight harvest is regime-fragile.
- For **country ETFs**, "overnight" (US close→US open) spans the *home market's actual trading session*, so the split is a different, more structural object: intraday (US hours) returns are S&P-driven, overnight returns carry home-market information; China-type funds show positive average overnight returns ([Overnight returns of stock indexes: evidence from ETFs and futures, IRFA 2017](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1059056016301563); [Schwab explainer on international ETF price discovery](https://www.schwabassetmanagement.com/content/international-etfs-and-price-discovery-mechanism)).
- Verdict: don't harvest overnight premium unconditionally; do use the overnight/intraday decomposition as *signal input* (it is the cleanest way to separate "home-market news" from "US beta echo" in daily country-ETF data — something ASADO can compute from its daily tables).

### 2.4 Flows, index rebalances — STRONG on direction (contrarian), MODERATE on tradability

- Frazzini & Lamont, *"Dumb money"* (JFE 2008): high mutual-fund inflows → low subsequent returns. ETF-era confirmations: ETFs with the largest inflows subsequently underperform peers ([FT coverage of studies](https://www-ft-com.ezproxy.cul.columbia.edu/content/c2cb1044-46f4-4ac9-a443-d75b97e99837); [Citywire on equity ETF flows as contrarian indicator](https://citywire.com/wealth-manager/news/how-to-use-equity-etf-flows-as-a-top-contrarian-indicator/a514900)). **Directly corroborates ASADO's ETF_FLOW_21D_Z contrarian sign (NW-t −2.2).**
- Index-reconstitution price pressure at the country level is real and large around MSCI reclassifications (frontier↔EM↔DM): NBER w23557, *"The Case of MSCI Country Reclassifications"* ([NBER](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w23557/w23557.pdf)); BIS WP 952 on passive-flow price effects ([BIS](https://www.bis.org/publ/work952.pdf)); hedge funds demonstrably front-run ETF rebalances ([SSRN 4324054](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/Delivery.cfm/4324054.pdf?abstractid=4324054&mirid=1)). But Greenwood & Sammon, *"The Disappearing Index Effect"* (HBS WP 23-025) show single-stock index effects have collapsed as arbitrage crowded in ([HBS](https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/23-025_563e45c6-df92-4d9c-ae05-608d4d0acab1.pdf)). For a $100k book: MSCI country reclassification events (Saudi 2019, Vietnam pending, Korea DM-watch, Poland) are infrequent but tradable event windows; routine quarterly adds/deletes are not worth chasing at the ETF level.

### 2.5 Cost reality for the 34-ETF universe

- Spreads are bimodal: SPY/QQQ/IWM/EWJ/EWZ/EWT/MCHI trade at ~1–5bps; tail names (VNM, EPHE, EPOL, ECH, EDEN, TUR in stress) at ~10–30bps, wider at open/close; premiums/discounts add noise on top ([Natixis](https://www.im.natixis.com/en-us/insights/portfolio-construction/2024/etf-cost-bid-ask-spread), [Vanguard](https://advisors.vanguard.com/insights/article/learn-how-to-navigate-etf-premiums-and-discounts)). Standard practitioner guidance: trade mid-session, never at open, use limit orders. ASADO's 5/10/25bps grid maps well: liquid half ≈ 5bps regime, full universe ≈ 10bps regime, stress/thin names ≈ 25bps (where nothing survives). Commission-free/IBKR-tiered commissions (~$0.35–1/trade) are immaterial at $100k except for minute-frequency designs, which the spread tail rules out anyway for half the universe.

## 3. Why country-timing models fail live — calibrated expectations

1. **Post-publication decay** — McLean & Pontiff (JF 2016): anomaly returns fall **~26% out-of-sample, ~58% post-publication** across 97 predictors ([summary](https://afajof.org/management/viewp.php?n=46984)). BUT Jacobs & Müller, *"Anomalies across the globe: Once public, no longer existent?"* (JFE 2020, 241 anomalies, 39 markets): the US is the **only** country with reliable post-publication decay; **none of 38 international markets** shows it — consistent with limits-to-arbitrage keeping international/country-level mispricing alive ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304405X19301618), [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2816490)). This is the single most encouraging outside fact for a country-selection book: the arbitrage capital that grinds away US stock anomalies largely hasn't shown up at the country-index level.
2. **Live fund evidence is brutal for discretionary/slow-signal country timing** — Morningstar's repeated studies: ~70% of tactical-allocation funds underperform a 60/40 index fund; over the last decade essentially **every** tactical allocation fund-of-funds underperformed, and manager timing decisions *subtracted* roughly half the return vs doing nothing ([Morningstar: Why Tactical-Allocation Funds Failed—Again](https://www.morningstar.com/funds/why-tactical-allocation-funds-failedagain), [Tactical Asset Allocation: Don't Try This at Home](https://www.morningstar.com/funds/tactical-asset-allocation-dont-try-this-home), [Institutional Investor](https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/2bw3ho98yb7yovg6xgd8g/portfolio/only-one-tactical-allocation-fund-has-managed-to-outperform-this-decade)). GVAL (Section 1.2) is the country-specific instance. The common failure mode: **regime concentration** — country bets are few and correlated (breadth illusion), so a decade-long regime (US>RoW) can swamp a genuine but small edge.
3. **Breadth math (Grinold–Kahn fundamental law)** — with 34 assets and daily independent bets, IR ≈ IC×√breadth×TC. A daily IC of 0.03–0.06 on ~30 names looks like huge breadth, but daily country bets are **not independent** (one global risk factor dominates; effective breadth per day is maybe 5–10, and signals autocorrelate day to day). Realistic ceiling for a *net-of-cost, long-only* implementation of a 0.03–0.05-IC daily cross-sectional country signal: **IR ~0.3–0.8 on the active return**, not the 2–3 gross long-short Sharpes seen in backtests ([fundamental law refs](https://investresolve.com/the-case-for-tactical-alpha-part-1-the-fundamental-law-of-active-management/); ReSolve makes exactly this country/asset-allocation breadth argument). ASADO's own long-only haircut ("captures roughly half the cross-sectional alpha") plus the ML-overfitting haircut (~30%, AFA 2024) should be applied multiplicatively to the combiner's stated ceiling.
4. **Crowding is factor-specific** — recent work (*"Not All Factors Crowd Equally"*, [arXiv 2512.11913](https://arxiv.org/html/2512.11913v1), Dec 2025) models heterogeneous alpha decay; fast microstructure-ish signals decay via crowding, slow macro signals via regime shifts. Plan for decay monitoring, not decay denial.
5. **Costs kill the middle** — Zaremba–Andreu (Section 1.1) + the practitioner spread evidence (Section 2.5) independently reproduce ASADO's cost-grid conclusion. The viable design space is a **barbell**: (a) fast (1-day) signals confined to the ≤5bps liquid half of the universe, and (b) slow (monthly–annual) tilts across everything.

## 4. Notable 2024–2026 developments

- **BlackRock launched CORO** (iShares International Country Rotation Active ETF, Dec 3 2024): active country rotation across non-US developed+EM using BlackRock model-portfolio + quant signals, implemented *by holding iShares country ETFs* ([BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241204096839/en/BlackRock-Introduces-International-Country-Rotation-Strategy-to-Active-ETF-Lineup), [fund page](https://www.ishares.com/us/products/340366/ishares-international-country-rotation-active-etf)). Significance: (a) validates the exact instrument set and strategy shape of this project; (b) a source of future crowding in the same ETFs; (c) a live benchmark to compare against.
- **AQR "Economic Trend"** formally published (SSRN Feb 2024) — see 1.4; the freshest institutional evidence that macro-trend country selection still works.
- **Cross-country factor momentum** (Economics Letters 2024) — see 1.1.
- **First ML paper on the country cross-section** (IRFA 2024) — see 1.6; plus the AFA 2024/25 result that ML strategy premia drop ~30% OOS from overfitting.
- **MSCI (2025), "Five Takeaways for Country Investing"** — 2025's historic non-US rally revived institutional interest in country-level dispersion; country dispersion in 2025 was the widest in a decade ([MSCI blog](https://www.msci.com/research-and-insights/blog-post/five-takeaways-for-country-investing-from-2025-historic-equity-shift)).
- **Valuation backdrop (mid-2026)**: US + India the most expensive markets globally; Hong Kong/China/UK among the cheapest even after the 2025 re-rating; Korea and Brazil closed much of their discount in 2025 ([Siblis CAPE by country](https://siblisresearch.com/data/cape-ratios-by-country/)). Relevant strategic context for a long-only book benchmarked to equal-weight-34.
- Sovereign credit corroboration: the **sovereign CDS term-structure slope predicts country equity returns** after standard controls, and inverted CDS curves flag deep, persistent equity underperformance ([JME 2018 term structure of CDS spreads](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304393218301740); [MDPI 2020 CDS–equity connectedness](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7390/8/10/1667)). Matches ASADO's CDS-inversion event study (−4.5% at +63d) and SOV_2S10S family. On ratings: the classic result (Brooks et al. 2004; Kaminsky–Schmukler) is downgrades hurt (~−1 to −2%, EM-concentrated) while upgrades produce no significant positive drift — consistent with ASADO's finding that even upgrades drift negative (MODERATE label from memory on exact citations).

## 6. Five best outside ideas worth importing

1. **Turn-of-month cash↔equity overlay (and trade-scheduling rule).** Hold the book fully invested through the [-1,+3] ToM window; do de-risking/rebalancing mid-month. International iShares evidence: 15–20bps/day in-window vs ~0 outside (Chen et al. 2015; McConnell–Xu 2008; 2026 JIMF re-test). Long-only+cash native, ~24 decisions/yr, per-window edge ≫ spreads. Cheapest first test: condition the existing brief's rebalance calendar on ToM.
2. **Fade the US-hours overshoot in non-synchronized country ETFs (Levy–Lieberman reversal).** On days the S&P moves sharply, Asia (and to a lesser degree Europe) ETFs over-move during US hours beyond fair beta; buy the overshot-down names near the US close, exit next day. This is the *correct tradable inversion* of ASADO's LL_LEADER_GAP trap, it's a 1-day hold in the liquid names (fits the proven ≤5bps regime), and it can be validated point-in-time from ASADO's daily tables + ETF price data.
3. **Peer-controlled NAV-premium/discount as an execution filter (Petajisto).** Compute each ETF's price deviation from a fair value implied by correlated ETFs/futures; execute planned buys only at/below fair value, delay at premium; opportunistically substitute (e.g., EWY cheap vs Asia basket). Converts a ~100bps documented mispricing band into cost reduction with **zero added turnover** — the single most retail-appropriate microstructure edge.
4. **A slow macro-trend + momentum(+value) sleeve at monthly rebalance (AQR Economic Trend; Long–Zaremba CLI; Zaremba–Andreu cost rescue).** Composite of OECD-CLI change, PMI trend, consensus-revision breadth, 12-1 price momentum, with a small CAPE-percentile tilt — top-N long-only, monthly, ~annual effective turnover per name. This is the end of the cost barbell ASADO's fast-signal digest leaves unoccupied, and it is the configuration with the strongest published net-of-cost support. Never run value alone (GVAL).
5. **Decay-calibrated position of the whole book.** Import the quantitative haircuts as design parameters: ~26% OOS / ~58% post-publication decay in the US but ~0 post-publication decay internationally (Jacobs–Müller — country-level anomalies live longer); ~30% OOS haircut for ML-selected composites; long-only ≈ half of long-short alpha; Grinold breadth math capping realistic net IR at ~0.3–0.8. Concretely: size strategies to be worth running *after* these haircuts, add a live paper-trading verification gate (the combiner's in-sample component selection is exactly the situation the AFA 2024 paper warns about), and monitor CORO/AQR-style institutional entry as a crowding tripwire.
